Write a Compelling Front End Developer Resume Objective or Summary

Use the resume summary

Got plenty of years bending HTML to your will? Use the resume summary. The summary statement, also known as a qualifications summary, is a brief paragraph highlighting your front end development experience, skills, and achievements. It also promotes you by listing one of your proudest achievements, using numbers, to prove you're competent.

Right Sample

Meticulous web developer with over 2 years of front end experience and passion for responsive website design and a firm believer in the mobile-first approach. W3C certified. Implemented new responsive website approach which increased mobile traffic by 20%.

Things to include in summary:

  • Job title and length
  • Specfic tech like responsive web design and progressive web app
  • Improvement demonstrated by data
Wrong Sample

As a web developer, I have many years of experience with CSS, JavaScript, and HTML. On top of that, my software development skills allowed me to build up my object-oriented programming abilities.

Problems in wrong sample:

  • Skills are not specific
  • Not having the points in right sample

Use a resume objective

What if you're a newbie to the world of professional front end development? A career objective is perfect for those writing a resume objective with no work experience in web development. Instead of your past work history, it shows your commitment by stating your career goals. It also supplies a numbered accomplishment to prove you're ready.

Right Sample

Recent bachelor's degree recipient with a background in web design and development. W3C web developer certification. Over 3 years of experience working on personal blogs, taking one from 1,000 monthly visitors to 50,000 visitors per month in just 1 year. Seeking to become the next front end developer for Jameson Communications.

Things to include in summary:

  • Degree and major (only list if related)
  • Personal experience with web development
  • Achievements demonstrated by data
  • Desire for new job
Wrong Sample

I just finished my bachelor's degree in information technology. I have what it takes to be a great web developer, front end or back end. Seeking a position on any web development team that'll have me!

Problems in wrong sample:

  • Skills are not specific
  • No experience and achievements mentioned
  • Not having the points in right sample

Resume Objective

A resume objective (or a career objective) is a heading statement of your resume, in which you describe your professional goals in the job you're applying for. A resume objective is usually 2-3 sentences long and should be placed at the top of your resume.

What Is an Objective on a Resume & When to Use It

What is a career objective on a resume?

A career objective on a resume is a top paragraph outlining relevant skills you've mastered so far and explaining what career you're seeking at a given company. Resume objectives should be used by candidates who don't have much work experience in the industry they're applying to: typically, entry-level job seekers or career changers.

When to use a resume objective?

  • People who are entering the job market for the first time.
  • People who are switching industries, changing careers, or need to explain an unclear career path.

Chronological Resume

What Is a Chronological Resume?

A chronological resume is a resume format that highlights your experience and key professional achievements. It's also known as the reverse-chronological resume because your latest position comes up top and the previous ones follow. Chronological resumes are easiest to scan for the ATS or Applicant Tracking Systems. That's what gives this format an additional edge in today's hiring if it is a ATS Resume.

Who Is the Chronological Resume Good For?

The reverse-chronological resume is most advantageous when:

  • You have several years of professional experience.
  • Your career progression has been steady, and preferably in one industry.
  • You don't have gaps in employment on a resume.

If you're making a recent college grad resume, for example, you only need to rearrange the order of your resume sections, and put the education resume section first. Just make sure the entries in each section follow the reverse-chronological order.

What's ATS?

ATS stands for Applicant Tracking System, and it's a piece of software that helps recruiters manage the recruitment process. 94% of those who incorporated some sort of ATS into their recruitment process say it has improved the hiring process. The ATS scores candidates' resumes according to certain preset criteria and shows the recruiter which candidates are the best fit for the advertised position. Gaps in Employment

How to Explain Gaps in Employment

Be Honest

In two lines or less, simply state what you were doing. See the employment gap example below. Just writing years in your work experience without months comes off as, well, trying to hide stuff.

Don't Fret EVERY Gap in Employment

  • Anything less than six months is generally OK. You still need to explain it.
  • Don't sweat gaps in employment 7-10 years ago. Again, you may need to explain, but an old gap isn't serious.

Be Prepared

  • Explain why you've got gaps in your resume.
  • Reassure the hiring manager why you won't be doing it again.
  • Share the value you picked up along the way.
  • Keep your explanation short. Have a finish line so you don't trail off into embarrassed silence.
